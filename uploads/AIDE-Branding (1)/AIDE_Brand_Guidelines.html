Section 01Brand Overview

Everything you need to understand what AIDE is, who it speaks to, and how it sounds.

What We Are

The AI-Driven Enterprise Institute (AIDE) is the definitive benchmarking and research body tracking how the world's largest companies adopt AI at the enterprise level. Through the AIDE Index — a proprietary scoring methodology across four pillars — alongside the AIDE Rankings, co-branded sector reports, and six specialized sub-brands, AIDE provides decision-grade intelligence on corporate AI maturity for boards, C-suites, investors, and consultancies.

Tagline

"Tracking the AI that counts."

This tagline anchors every piece of AIDE communication. It signals our focus on enterprise AI adoption that drives real business outcomes — not hype, not research prototypes, not consumer apps. Use it consistently across all channels.

Brand Voice

AIDE's voice is built around four qualities. Every piece of content — from social posts to PDF reports — should reflect these:

Authoritative
Data-first, benchmark-driven. We let the numbers lead.
Clear
Precise language, no jargon. Accessible to board-level audiences.
Measured
Confident without hyperbole. We don't predict — we measure.
Forward-looking
Tracking where enterprise AI is going, not just where it's been.

Audience

Our primary audiences are:

  • Board directors and C-suite executives evaluating their company's AI readiness
  • Management consultancies (Big Four, boutique strategy firms) using AIDE data in client engagements
  • Institutional investors factoring AI maturity into portfolio decisions
  • Enterprise AI leaders benchmarking against peers

All brand communication should feel like a briefing for a senior executive: substantive, concise, visually clean, and backed by data.

Usage Rules

Do

  • Use the horizontal logo as the primary logo on light backgrounds
  • Use the white logo on Dark Navy (#1B2541) backgrounds
  • Use the standalone A mark for favicons, app icons, and small-format contexts
  • Maintain clear space equal to the "A" mark height on all sides
  • Use provided PNG files at their original proportions

Don't

  • Distort, rotate, skew, or stretch the logo
  • Recolor the logo — use only the provided versions
  • Place the logo on busy, patterned, or photographic backgrounds
  • Add drop shadows, outlines, glows, or effects
  • Rearrange logo elements or change the wordmark typeface
  • Recreate the logo from scratch — always use the master files
  • Use the logo smaller than minimum size

Section 04Typography

AIDE uses a single typeface family across all materials. Consistency in type is as important as consistency in color.

Typeface

Primary: Camera Plain Variable

Fallback stack: ui-sans-serif, system-ui, sans-serif

No other typefaces should be used in any AIDE materials. Camera Plain Variable is the same typeface used on the live aideinstitute.com site. If unavailable in a specific environment, the fallback stack ensures visual consistency.

Section 08Asset Generation

All social, email, and OG assets are generated programmatically from the geometric A mark using a single Python script.

Quick start:
cd ~/aide/branding && python3 generate_assets.py

This regenerates all 11 assets in place. Logo files in logos/png/ and icons/ are never touched.
ItemDetail
Script~/aide/branding/generate_assets.py
DependencyPillow (PIL) — pip install Pillow
Source markicons/favicon.png (geometric "A" lettermark)
Output directoriessocial/linkedin/, social/x/, social/ (OG), email/
Total assets11 (4 LinkedIn + 4 X + 1 OG + 2 email)
IdempotentYes — safe to re-run at any time; overwrites existing files
For designers: The script handles template layouts only. To create a final social post with real data, open the generated template in your editor and replace the placeholder text (e.g., "[STAT]", "[ Quote text goes here ]") with actual content. The templates ensure correct dimensions, brand colors, and logo placement.
